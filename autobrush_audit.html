🔴 High Priority Issues
HIGH
Cart CTA Button Says "ADD SUBSCRIPTON" (Typo)
#01
›
Issue Description

The main add-to-cart button across multiple product cards reads "ADD SUBSCRIPTON" — missing the letter 'I' in SUBSCRIPTION. This is on the primary conversion action and damages brand credibility immediately.

Spotted on the homepage product carousel and the /collections/all page. Every product card shows "ADD SUBSCRIPTON" as the CTA. At 347K monthly visitors this is hurting trust at scale.
Why It Matters

Customers notice typos in CTAs. It signals carelessness and reduces conversion confidence — especially for a premium-priced product ($99–$150+). First impressions on the CTA button are critical.

Recommended Fix
Fix the button text in Shopify theme code or section settings Search theme for "SUBSCRIPTON" and replace with "SUBSCRIPTION" Audit all CTA buttons sitewide for copy errors
HIGH
Duplicate Product Listing — Same Review Count on ALL Products
#02
›
Issue Description

Every single product on the site — from the Brush Head ($25) to the Tongue Scraper ($13) — shows the exact same review count: 4.3 / 5.0 with 841 total reviews. This is almost certainly a template/code bug where the review widget is pulling from the wrong product or a global variable.

Confirmed on: Brush Head, Foam Toothpaste Sampler, Automatic Tongue Scraper — all display identical "841 total reviews" and "4.3/5.0" rating. This looks fake to savvy shoppers.
Why It Matters

Social proof is one of the top conversion drivers. When reviews look copy-pasted across all products, it destroys trust — especially since review apps like Judge.me and Bazaarvoice are installed. The data is there, it's just not displaying correctly per product.

Recommended Fix
Audit the review app (Judge.me/Junip) snippet in the product card template Ensure product-specific handle/ID is passed to the review widget Test on staging before deploying the fix live
HIGH
Duplicate Image Rendering — Same Image Loaded 20+ Times Per Product
#03
›
Issue Description

In the HTML source, each product in the carousel has its thumbnail image rendered 20–24 times in the DOM (confirmed via the raw HTML). For example, the brush head image URL appears 24 times and the replenishment pack image appears 24 times. This creates excessive DOM nodes and likely slows page render.

Example: brush-head_single.png appears 12 times and brush-head_single_1.png appears 6 more times for the same product card. The replenishment_pack_kids.png appears 24 times in one section alone.
Why It Matters

Page speed directly impacts conversion rate and SEO rankings. Duplicate image nodes bloat the DOM, waste bandwidth, and can confuse the browser's rendering engine. Google's Core Web Vitals penalize this kind of bloat.

Recommended Fix
Audit the carousel/slider section — likely a loop bug in the Liquid template Use Shopify's lazy loading for product images Run PageSpeed Insights and address LCP/CLS issues
HIGH
Missing Hero Section / Weak Above-the-Fold Experience
#04
›
Issue Description

The homepage jumps straight into a product carousel without a strong hero statement. For a product that needs explanation (U-shaped brush, 30-second clean), there's no clear above-the-fold value proposition with a hero image/video that converts cold traffic — especially since they spend heavily on paid ads.

Why It Matters

Cold traffic from paid ads lands on this homepage with zero context. Without a powerful hero answering "What is this? Why do I need it? What makes it different?" — bounce rates will be high. The brand has strong clinical proof (ADA-accepted, 5.1x cleaner) that should be front and center.

Recommended Fix
Add a video hero showing the brush in action (30-second demo) Bold headline: "Clean your whole mouth in 30 seconds" Add ADA badge + clinical proof icons above the fold Single primary CTA above the fold ("Shop Now" or "Start Here")
HIGH
Product Variant Confusion — No "Sold Out" Clarity, No Size Guidance
#05
›
Issue Description

Products like the Brush Head require customers to select Pack (2-month, 6-month, 12-month) AND Brush Size (Age 3-5, Age 6-8, Age 9-12, Women, Men, XL). Yet the button says "Sold out" without indicating WHICH variant is sold out. A customer has no way to know if the product they want is available without clicking through everything.

The CTA button reads "ADD SUBSCRIPTON — Sold out" as a fallback, but no individual variant availability is shown. The "Size Guide" option is buried inside the variant selector rather than linked clearly.
Why It Matters

Variant confusion is a massive cart abandonment driver. If a customer can't quickly understand what's available in their size, they leave. The size guide should be prominently linked near the selector, not buried.

Recommended Fix
Show per-variant availability (cross out sold-out sizes) Move Size Guide to a visible modal link next to the variant selector Add a "Notify Me" button for sold-out variants (Back-in-Stock app)
